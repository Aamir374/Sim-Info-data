How to Check SIM Owner Details in Pakistan (2026 Guide)

Jan 28, 2026 6 min read

In Pakistan, verifying the ownership of a mobile number is not just a curiosity—it's often a safety necessity. Whether you’re dealing with unknown callers, verifying a used SIM purchase, or simply checking your own records, knowing the official methods is crucial.

In this guide, we will walk you through the official and most reliable ways to check SIM owner details in 2026, including SMS services, web portals, and utility tools.

1. The Official 668 SMS Service

The Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA) provides a primary service to check the total number of SIMs registered against a CNIC. This is the first step in verifying ownership.

  • Open your mobile SMS application.
  • Type your **CNIC Number** (without dashes).
  • Send it to **668**.
  • You will receive a reply showing the count of SIMs on Jazz, Telenor, Zong, and Ufone.

Pro Tip:

If you find more SIMs than you actually own, visit the nearest customer service center of that network immediately to block unauthorized numbers.

2. Checking SIM Owner Name via 7911

If you need the specific name of the owner for a SIM you currently possess, most networks support the 7911 service. Simply send a blank message or the word "MNP" to **7911** from the SIM you want to check. You will receive the owner's name and CNIC status.

4. Essential Safety Precautions

While checking SIM details, always remember to respect privacy laws. Unauthorized tracking or harassment is a punishable offense under the **Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act (PECA)**. Only use these tools for legitimate verification purposes.

Staying informed about your digital identity is the best way to prevent fraud. We recommend checking your registered SIM count at least once every six months.
