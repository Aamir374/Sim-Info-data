CNIC Information System: How to Check SIMs on CNIC (2026)

Feb 03, 2026 5 min read

Every citizen in Pakistan is allowed a maximum of five SIM cards across all mobile networks. Managing your digital identity starts with knowing exactly how many SIMs are registered against your Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC).

In this comprehensive guide, we'll cover the latest 2026 official methods to verify your mobile connections and ensure no unauthorized numbers are active under your name.

1. PTA's 668 SMS Information System

This is the most widely used and reliable method. PTA has made it incredibly simple to get a breakdown of your registered SIMs by network.

  • Go to your mobile's messaging app.
  • Create a new message and type your **13-digit CNIC number** (no dashes/spaces).
  • Send this message to **668**.
  • Wait for a few seconds to receive a detailed reply.

The reply will show the total number of active SIMs on Jazz, Telenor, Zong, and Ufone registered under your CNIC.

Standard Charges:

Sending an SMS to 668 costs approximately PKR 2.00 + tax per inquiry.

2. Online SIM Information Portal

For those who prefer a web-based interface, PTA provides a real-time portal. This is especially useful if you don't have credit on your phone or want to check from a desktop.

  1. Visit the official PTA SIM Information Website (cnic.sims.pk).
  2. Enter your CNIC number in the required field.
  3. Complete the CAPTCHA verification.
  4. Click the **Submit** button to see your status instantly.

3. What to do if you find unauthorized SIMs?

If the count received from 668 or the portal is higher than the number of SIM cards you actually possess, you might be a victim of identity theft. Follow these steps immediately:

  • Identify which network has the extra SIMs.
  • Visit the nearest **Customer Service Center** of that specific network.
  • Take your original CNIC with you.
  • Requested the representative to block and cancel all unauthorized numbers.

Warning:

Using a SIM registered in someone else's name or allowing others to use your registered SIM for illegal activities is a serious crime under the Pakistan Penal Code and PECA.

4. Frequency of Checking

We recommend verifying your SIM count at least **twice a year**. With the rise of digital services and biometric verification, keeping your CNIC data clean is a vital part of your personal security.
