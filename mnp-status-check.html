MNP Status: How to Check if Your SIM is Ported (2026)

Feb 10, 2026 6 min read

Mobile Number Portability (MNP) has revolutionized the telecom industry in Pakistan by allowing users to switch between networks while keeping their original phone number. But how do you know if a number has been ported, and which network it currently belongs to?

This comprehensive guide will explain everything you need to know about MNP status checking in 2026, including official methods and practical tips.

1. What is Mobile Number Portability (MNP)?

Mobile Number Portability is a service regulated by the Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA) that allows mobile subscribers to change their network operator without changing their mobile number.

For example, if you have a Jazz number (starting with 0300) but want to switch to Zong for better coverage or pricing, you can port your number to Zong while keeping the same 0300 number.

Key Benefits of MNP:

  • Keep your existing mobile number when switching networks
  • Choose the best network based on coverage, pricing, or services
  • Avoid the hassle of informing contacts about a new number
  • Increase competition among telecom providers, leading to better services

2. How to Check MNP Status (Current Network)

There are several official methods to verify which network a mobile number currently belongs to:

Method 1: SMS to 667 (Universal MNP Check)

This is the most reliable and official method provided by PTA:

  1. Open your SMS application
  2. Type the mobile number you want to check (e.g., 03001234567)
  3. Send it to 667
  4. You will receive a reply showing the current network operator

Example Response:

"The number 03001234567 is currently on Zong Network (Ported from Jazz)"

Method 2: Call 310 (Network-Specific)

Some networks provide their own MNP checking service:

  • Call 310 from your mobile
  • Follow the automated voice instructions
  • Enter the number you want to check
  • The system will tell you the current network

3. How to Port Your Number to Another Network

If you want to switch networks while keeping your number, follow these steps:

  1. Check Eligibility: Your SIM must be active for at least 90 days on the current network
  2. Clear Outstanding Bills: Ensure all dues are paid to your current operator
  3. Request Porting Code: Send an SMS with your CNIC number to your current operator's porting service
  4. Visit New Operator: Take your CNIC and porting code to the new network's customer service center
  5. Complete Biometric Verification: Provide biometric verification at the franchise
  6. Wait for Activation: Porting typically completes within 24-48 hours

Porting Charges (2026):

The standard MNP porting fee in Pakistan is approximately PKR 250-300, though this may vary by operator and promotional offers.

4. Common MNP Myths Debunked

Let's clear up some common misconceptions about Mobile Number Portability:

  • Myth: "Porting will delete my contacts and data"
    Reality: Your contacts, messages, and data remain intact. Only the network provider changes.
  • Myth: "I can port my number anytime"
    Reality: You must wait 90 days after activation before porting.
  • Myth: "Porting is instant"
    Reality: The process takes 24-48 hours to complete.
  • Myth: "I'll lose my number during porting"
    Reality: Your number remains yours throughout the process, though there may be brief downtime.

5. Why Check MNP Status?

Knowing the current network of a mobile number is useful for several reasons:

  • Cost Savings: On-net calls (same network) are often cheaper than off-net calls
  • Package Selection: Choose the right calling package based on the recipient's network
  • Business Communication: Optimize business communication costs by knowing client networks
  • Verification: Verify if a number has been ported before making assumptions about the network

6. Important Things to Remember

  • You cannot port prepaid numbers to postpaid or vice versa directly
  • Corporate/company numbers may have different porting rules
  • Your current operator cannot refuse porting if you meet all requirements
  • After porting, your old SIM card will become inactive
  • You can port back to your original network after 90 days if needed

Pro Tip:

Before porting, compare network coverage in your area, pricing plans, and customer service quality. The cheapest option isn't always the best if coverage is poor in your location.

Make Informed Decisions

Mobile Number Portability gives you the freedom to choose the best network for your needs. Use the 667 SMS service to check MNP status anytime, and don't hesitate to switch if another network offers better value.
